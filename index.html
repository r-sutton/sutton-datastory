SOCIAL MEDIA APP USAGE

At the beginning of the semester, I was able to track my data usage on four different apps: Facebook, Snapchat, GroupMe, and Twitter. I decided on these four because they were the apps I utilized the most often to communicate with my friends and colleagues. On this webpage, you'll find information and interpretations of my data collection. Enjoy!

Through this data collection I was able to discover which apps I used on a more professional basis and which I used on a informal level. It was interesting to track my various interactions with people and how I used apps versus texting to get in contact with them.

Data Explained Video

This video was based around my data collection. I found that a lot of my data was split between personal and professional relationships. This made me question the concept of a private versus personal life on the internet, and how using different apps to connect with certain types of people could result in potential employers finding personal profiles instead of professional ones through app linking.

Many social media apps allow you to link your apps together (such as Instagram and Facebook to post simultaneously and using Facebook log-ins on other apps), which can be convenient, but also puts users at risk of anyone they're connected with on professional-use apps finding their profiles (and potentially following/friending them) on their personal-use apps. For me, this would be someone on my Facebook or GroupMe I know professionally finding my Twitter or Snapchat through my other logins.

In the video, I discuss the difference between personal and professional uses for social media, and how the need for privacy on all apps is vital to all people who utilize social media sites.
