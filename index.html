SOCIAL MEDIA APP USAGE

At the beginning of the semester, I was able to track my data usage on four different apps: Facebook, Snapchat, GroupMe, and Twitter. I decided on these four because they were the apps I utilized the most often to communicate with my friends and colleagues. Through this data collection I was able to discover which apps I used on a more professional basis and which I used on a informal level. It was interesting to track my various interactions with people and how I used apps versus texting to get in contact with them.

From there, this semester, I have created a data visualization of my data (shown on the right) and a video explaining how professional versus personal social media usage is affected by app linking. This data collection has made me much more aware of my social media use and the possible consequences of misusing certain apps. Below, you'll find more specific information regarding my usage of each app.





Facebook

Throughout the week, I sent the most messages on Facebook. I work for the Collegiate Times, Virginia Tech's newspaper, and we use Facebook to communicate with all of the sections when we edit articles. Due to this, my interactions were all more professional than personal. Although there were occassional messages sent to people on an informal level (to friends) a vast majority were work-related.

Snapchat

Throughout the week I solely used Snapchat to communicate with my friends. It was one of the more informal apps, and all of my conversations were about funny or random things â€” never really anything serious. I never spoke to someone I knew only on a professional level through Snapchat, in fact, I don't even have any of them added on my Snapchat.

GroupMe

GroupMe was a bit of a mix, but, like Facebook, was used mostly for professional communication. I am a Hokie Undergraduate Group Leader for the Department of Communication and a Features Editor for Her Campus, both of which utilize GroupMe to communicate. Also, all of my large group messages for my sorority, Chi Omega, are on the GroupMe app. The relationship I have with most of the people is either through work or extra-curriculars.

Twitter

Twitter, like Snapchat, was used only for messaging friends. Throughout the week, I discovered that I use Twitter a lot less than I thought I did, which surprised me. However, I figured that the only people I'd be communicating with on the app were my friends because it's a very informal form of communication, whereas Facebook and GroupMe can be professional if used in the right context.


Data Explained Video

This video was based around my data collection. I found that a lot of my data was split between personal and professional relationships. This made me question the concept of a private versus personal life on the internet, and how using different apps to connect with certain types of people could result in potential employers finding personal profiles instead of professional ones through app linking.

In the video, I discuss the difference between personal and professional uses for social media, and how the need for privacy on all apps is vital to all people who utilize social media sites.
